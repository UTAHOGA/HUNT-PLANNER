Professional, Fair, Transparent

The goal of this service is to help hunting clients find qualified Utah outfitters without hidden favoritism. Listings should be earned through clear standards, and hunt matches should follow neutral criteria that a visitor can understand.

Recommended operating rule: if U.O.G.A. or the site owner is also listed as an outfitter, that listing must follow the same published placement rules as every other qualified operator.

Baseline Vetting Standard

  • Utah licensing and registration status verified
  • Insurance and contact information current
  • Species and hunt areas clearly identified
  • Service type disclosed: guided, drop camp, access, or consulting
  • Public-facing business information reviewed for consistency

Referral Fairness

  • Match outfitters by species, hunt unit, and service fit first
  • Display all qualified vetted matches, not just one
  • If sponsored or featured placement exists, label it clearly
  • Use neutral sorting or rotation for equally qualified listings
  • Apply the same rule set to every outfitter, including your own listing

Client Trust Signals

Visitors should understand why a given outfitter appears for a hunt. Good labels include species served, units served, guide type, and whether the match is direct or broader regional coverage.

Complete Outfitter List

The next step is a normalized master outfitter list with one clean record per operator. That master list can feed both the map match system and a public directory page.
